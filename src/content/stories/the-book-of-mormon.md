Plato said: "I'm trying to think, don't confuse me with facts." When Congress was considering whether to impeach President Nixon, one legislator purportedly said, "My mind is made up. Don't confuse me with facts!"

LADY AT CHECKOUT DESK: "So how much for this book?"

ME: "It's a paperback, so it's a dollar."

LADY: "Well, you know it's the BOOK OF MORMON. They give these away."

ME: "I'm sure they do."

LADY: "So you want me to pay for it even though the Mormons give them away?"

ME: "Yes, ma'am. The book doesn't belong to ME. It's for sale for $1."

LADY: "Well, I want the book. But I can't believe you want me to pay for a book that the Mormons give away for free."

ME: "Ma'am, I am not a Mormon. My clients are not Mormons. There is nothing for free at an estate sale. The book is one dollar. Do you want it or not?"

LADY: "I only want it if it's free..."

ME: "Next in line, please!"
