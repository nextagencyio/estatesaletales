A "Good Luck Charm" is an item or activity that supposedly gives a little boost to whatever part luck plays in what you're doing, whether personally or professionally. I've heard of lucky bowling shirts, wearing lucky cufflinks to sales interviews, picking four-leaf clovers, etc. When I was a kid, elementary school boys might carry a rabbit's foot in their blue jeans pocket for good luck — wasn't that lucky for the rabbit, of course.

Sitting at the checkout desk at estate sales, my hands were on display as I counted the money, wrote receipts, etc. I religiously made time to get a fresh manicure on the day before the start of every sale. My good luck charm!

My regular nail salon had a small fire that closed the business for two weeks. My estate sale was starting the next day, so a good luck manicure was mandatory. With some small amount of trepidation, I stopped in at a new salon next closest to my house.

YOUNG LADY AT DESK: "Hello! Long time, no see." I had never been there before but assumed it was the standard "Long time no see" I get at my regular place. "No work for you today?"

ME: "No, not today. I'd like a dip manicure and a spa pedicure, please."

LADY: "Rose will do for you. She is very good, and you will like it."

Generally, the ladies and men who work at nail salons I've been to are on the young side. They are typically hard-working Vietnamese who speak to each other in their native language, and when they speak their second language, English, there are some things that translate well and some that don't. Either way, I love the pampering and relaxing. When they talk amongst themselves, I fantasize they're saying, "I love this lady. She is so beautiful and a good tipper. I wish she would come every day!"

My lady Rose came out from the back room. She was older, maybe in her 60s, and her English was pretty good. As usual, she told me to put my feet in the warm water foot bath. After that this was pretty much our dialogue:

ROSE: "Ohh, your feet are rough. Do you wear shoes?"

ME: "Yes, I do wear shoes. I was wearing shoes when I walked in here."

ROSE: "Let me see your hands. Ohh, do you bite your nails?"

ME: "No, I don't bite my nails. They are not very strong, though, so they break. Do you have something you can put on them to make them stronger?"

ROSE (Moving closer to my face with my hand in hers and speaking loudly): "You want your nails to be strong? DON'T BITE THEM!"

Things were quiet after that, because I wasn't about to offer up any other avenue for criticism. But it didn't matter. As I was walking out:

ROSE: "What happened to your hair?"

ME: "My hair?"

ROSE: "What happened to your hair?"

ME: "Well, it is getting thinner..."

ROSE: "Ohh. You're old, so it's falling out."

Rose must have had a good luck charm in her pocket. Because lucky for Rose, I had already handed over her tip...
