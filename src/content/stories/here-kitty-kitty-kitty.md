In September 2021, a lady phoned Dallas liquidator Tom Keener from her home in San Antonio. Her dad lived alone in Dallas, and she was going to make a quick trip to pick him up and bring him back to San Antonio to live with her.

She told Tom she was concerned her dad's poor memory had gone from very minimal decline to very serious decline, and he didn't need to live alone anymore. She said she and her dad would pack his clothes and some paperwork, but pretty much everything else in the house would be for Tom to deal with and hopefully sell. She and her dad would pack up and be gone in a day. They would not have time to meet with Tom but had heard good things about his estate sale business, so they were happy to leave things in his capable hands.

You know how it typically is when an old man lives alone — the place was a horror! The daughter was happy to grab up her dad and drive away, fast! The client left a key for Tom under the doormat, and Tom told her he and his team could get started in about a week.

After the old gentleman had been with his daughter for about five days, they were eating dinner in their recliners and the conversation went like this:

DAD: "Honey, this is a good dinner. But where is MY CAT?"

DAUGHTER: "What did you say?"

DAD: "I said dinner is good."

DAUGHTER: "Did you say something about A CAT?"

DAD: "Yes, where is MY CAT?"

DAUGHTER: "What CAT?"

DAD: "My CAT! My CAT! Where is she? Maybe she's under the bed."

DAUGHTER: "What bed, Daddy?"

DAD: "Well, my bed, of course."

DAUGHTER: "Daddy, did you have a cat living in the house?"

DAD: "Yes, Honey. Where is she now?"

DAUGHTER: "Daddy, she must still be in the house — and you moved out five days ago!"

Tom got a frantic call from the daughter. He went to the house early the next morning. Easy to see that the feline had been there alone — and she was not happy! Of course, the house smelled horrible, with cat pee and poop everywhere. And the cat had clawed the drapes, all the upholstered furniture, and most of the carpet to shreds.

The house was in such disarray when the daughter came to collect her dad, that she hadn't noticed two small bowls for food and water pushed just under his bed. And since she and her dad had entered and exited through the front door, she hadn't seen the collection of bowls and cat food cans sitting outside the back door. There was no litter box inside the house because the cat stayed outside except to come inside and eat at nighttime, then sleep under the old gentleman's bed. With the extra noise in the house while the daughter was there, the cat just kept quiet under the bed and got left behind!

Tom found the unhappy kitty. It helped that he opened a can of tuna fish! He fed the cat and ushered her outside. The next morning when he came to check on things, there were SIX obviously feral cats hanging out around the side porch. Tom talked to the lady next door. The old gentle man had been putting out food for the neighborhood cats for a really long time. The neighbor said Tom was likely to see 10 CATS lining up at the daily porch buffet!

From this point, to shorten the tale, Tom did the estate sale as planned. Needless to say, the carpet soaked in cat pee had to be steam cleaned first! An assortment of cats and kittens came and went in and out of the house anytime a customer opened a door. Basically, a cat circus. Have you heard the expression, "It's impossible, like trying to herd cats"? Truth!

When the sale was over, good-hearted Tom called a cat rescue group. They set out humane traps to catch the cats, do spaying/neutering, then hopefully get them adopted. The next-door neighbor called the daughter and volunteered to give the owner's cat a forever home. This was great, as the daughter didn't want a cat — and thankfully her sweet dad had already forgotten about it. Sometimes a little dementia is a blessing!

All in a day's work for estate liquidators!!
