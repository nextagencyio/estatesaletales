Two guys walked in the door at one of my sales a few years back. They were dressed in unkempt clothes, hair a little greasy-looking, but I probably wouldn't have noticed them at all in the group of people who walked in. Except they immediately reminded me of the little dirty "Pig Pen" character in the Charlie Brown cartoons, the little boy who always had a cloud of dust following him. These guys walked in with a cloud following them, too. But it wasn't dust. Definitely smoke with a strong, lingering stench — smelled for all the world like SKUNK. The new customers did attract attention from other shoppers as they walked around with the smell attached to them. I heard a woman say, "Whoa, that is some good African weed." Oh. Got it.

The weedy guys got lost in the crowded house, but eventually made their way back to the front room where I was doing checkout. By then I had heard one guy say some inappropriate things to some women in one room of the house, so I was anxious for them to leave. But that same guy spied a beautiful grandfather clock against the wall by my desk. "How much is the clock, Lady?" "It's $1,400," I said. "Wow, I really want that clock," he said. "Great," I tried to say with confidence, "I'll mark it SOLD for you. It's $1,400 cash." "Oh, cash? I'll have to go to the bank." "That's fine," I said, "we'll hold it for you for 30 minutes." At that juncture, I would have bet 100 American dollars that we would never see these two guys again after they supposedly were leaving to get $1,400 cash from the bank.

Surprise, surprise, it took two hours, but the two street characters came back to the sale with $1,400 in crisp $100 bills. (Just to be safe, I did run the counterfeit detector pen over a few of them.) Like I said, estate liquidators can overlook a lot of crazy and some skunk weed smell when those crazy pot-smoking customers are spending money!
