**Product:** Book of true, funny stories from 20+ years in estate liquidation
**Goal:** Drive book awareness and sales through entertaining, SEO-optimized content
**Audience:** People fascinated by the weird, funny, and human side of estate sales

## Page Specifications

### Homepage (/)

**Purpose:** Hook visitors, showcase the book, drive to /book or /stories

**Sections:**
1. **Hero** — "True stories from 20 years of estate liquidation" + book cover image + "Get the Book" CTA
2. **The Hook** — "After clearing out 1,000+ homes, you see things. A snake under the bed. A goose blocking traffic. A naked man in the kitchen. These are the stories we couldn't make up."
3. **Featured Stories** — 3-4 story cards from /stories with excerpts
4. **About the Author** — Brief bio with photo, link to /about
5. **From the Book** — Sample excerpt or short story
6. **Newsletter Signup** — "Get a new story every week"
7. **As Seen In** — Press logos (if any), reviews/blurbs

**SEO:**
- Title: "Estate Sale Tales — True Stories from 20 Years of Estate Liquidation"
- Meta: "Funny, bizarre, and heartwarming stories from a career clearing out 1,000+ homes. Snakes, hidden treasures, awkward encounters, and everything in between."
- Schema: Book, Organization, WebSite

### Book Page (/book)

**Purpose:** Sell the book

**Sections:**
1. **Book hero** — Cover image, title, tagline, price, buy buttons
2. **Description** — What the book is about, what readers will find
3. **Sample stories** — 2-3 short excerpts
4. **Reviews/blurbs** — Quotes from reviewers or early readers
5. **Buy links** — Amazon, Barnes & Noble, Bookshop.org, signed copies
6. **FAQ** — "Is this fiction?" (No), "How many stories?" etc.

**SEO:**
- Title: "Estate Sale Tales: The Book — Buy Now"
- Schema: Book with offers, reviews, author

### About Page (/about)

**Purpose:** Build author credibility and personal connection

**Sections:**
1. **Author photo + name**
2. **Background** — 20+ years in estate liquidation, 1,000+ homes cleared
3. **Why the book** — "After 20 years of telling these stories at dinner parties, people kept saying 'you should write a book'"
4. **The mission** — Sharing the human side of estate sales
5. **Contributors** — Other estate sale pros who contributed stories
6. **Contact** — Email for media/speaking

**SEO:**
- Title: "About — Estate Sale Tales"
- Schema: Person (author)

### Stories Index (/stories)

**Purpose:** Browse all stories, organized by category

**Layout:** Grid of story cards with category filters

**Categories:**
- Weird Finds
- Animal Encounters
- Awkward Moments
- Valuable Discoveries
- Behind the Scenes
- Shopper Shenanigans

**SEO:**
- Title: "Estate Sale Stories — Funny, Bizarre & True"
- Meta: "True stories from estate sale professionals. Weird finds, animal encounters, valuable discoveries, and the things you can't make up."

### Individual Story Pages (/stories/[slug])

**Template elements:**
- Story title
- Category badge
- Read time
- Author/contributor attribution
- Full story content
- "From the Book" badge (if it's in the book)
- Related stories (3)
- Book CTA: "This story is from Estate Sale Tales. [Get the Book →]"
- Social share buttons (these stories are shareable)
- Newsletter signup

**SEO per story:**
- Unique title + meta description
- Article schema with author
- OG image (could be a branded template with story title)

### Guide Pages (/guides/[slug])

**Template elements:**
- Guide title
- Read time
- Full educational content (with anecdotes woven in)
- Related stories sidebar
- Book CTA
- FAQ section with schema

## Story Content Guidelines

### Tone
- Conversational, warm, funny — like telling the story at a dinner party
- First person ("I" or "We") from the professional's perspective
- Respectful of the people involved — never mean-spirited
- Details that make it real (city names, seasons, sensory descriptions)
- Short paragraphs, punchy pacing, strong openings

### Structure
Every story should follow this pattern:
1. **Hook** — Start in the middle of the action (2-3 sentences max)
2. **Setup** — Brief context: what kind of sale, what kind of house
3. **The Moment** — The funny/weird/dramatic thing that happened
4. **The Aftermath** — How it was resolved, what happened next
5. **The Takeaway** — Optional reflection or one-liner that wraps it up

### Length
- Short stories: 500-800 words (quick reads, good for social sharing)
- Long stories: 1,000-1,500 words (more complex narratives)
- Listicle stories: 1,500-2,500 words ("10 Weirdest Things We've Found")

### Attribution
- Stories from other estate sale professionals should credit them: "Story by [Name], [Company], [City, State]"
- Include a note: "Have an estate sale story? [Submit yours →]"

---

## Guide Content Guidelines

### Tone
- Still conversational and warm, but more informational
- Weave in anecdotes from the book/stories — never dry or textbook
- "Here's what the internet says... here's what it's actually like"
- Use the author's 20 years of experience as the differentiator

### Structure
1. **Direct answer** — Answer the question in the first paragraph
2. **The real story** — What it's actually like from the inside
3. **Practical details** — Steps, costs, tips
4. **Anecdote** — A story that illustrates the point
5. **Book CTA** — "For more stories from the estate sale world..."

### Length
- 1,500-2,500 words per guide
- Include FAQ section at the bottom (for featured snippets)

## Technical Requirements

### Framework
- Astro (static HTML for SEO, same as dropjs-marketing)
- Content collections for stories and guides (markdown with frontmatter)
- Responsive design — stories should read well on phones

### Structured Data (JSON-LD)
- **Homepage:** Book, WebSite, Organization
- **Book page:** Book with offers, reviews
- **About:** Person (author)
- **Stories:** Article with author, datePublished
- **Guides:** Article with FAQ schema
- **All pages:** BreadcrumbList

### Design Direction
- Warm, inviting — not corporate. Think "coffee table book" aesthetic
- Light theme (unlike dropjs dark theme) — this is a consumer/book site
- Serif or friendly sans-serif typography for readability
- Book cover prominently featured
- Story cards with category badges and read times
- Social share buttons on every story

### Newsletter
- Replace TinyLetter (discontinued) with ConvertKit or Buttondown
- Signup on homepage, /newsletter, and after every story
- Weekly email with one new story + book promotion
